3S Battery Management Unit for MRI-Compatible Display System

A custom-designed 3S battery management system for powering a tablet in an MRI environment. The design focuses on safety, reliability, and thermal management.

1. Component Selection: Boost Converter to Linear Regulator (LM7805)

Transition from boost converter (inductor hazard in MRI) to linear regulator:
sacrificing efficiency for safety
removing magnetic components from LM7805 module
designing custom heatsink for 3-hour continuous operation.

2. 3-Cell Protection Circuit

Microcontroller/Processor Selection

Selection and configuration of the 5V microcontroller unit for system control.

3. Custom Thermal Management with Aluminum Heatsink

The aluminum heatsink was designed to dissipate heat from the linear regulator, ensuring safe operation.

4. Thermal Camera Testing: With and Without Heatsink

Thermal imaging shows a significant temperature reduction when using the custom heatsink.

Note: Trade-offs considered between MRI safety requirements, thermal performance, and cost efficiency.

NRZ vs. RZ Signal Performance Analysis

In ELEC 486 optical communications project, I compared the performance of Non-Return-to-Zero (NRZ) and Return-to-Zero (RZ) signaling schemes under varying attenuation levels using eye diagram analysis and Q-factor measurements in OptiWave's OptiSystem simulation environment.

Tool: OptiWave OptiSystem 18 (industry-standard optical network design software)

Methodology:

  • Varied attenuation levels (95‚Äì118 dB) and measured received power
  • Analyzed signal integrity using Eye Diagram Analyzer and Q-factor calculations

1.Signal Integrity Analysis: NRZ vs RZ Under Varying Attenuation

At High Received Power (Pr = 0 dBm):
RZ: Eye diagram remained wide open at 95 dB attenuation
NRZ: Eye only partially open at 98 dB attenuation
Key Findings: RZ provides 3 dB better attenuation tolerance but requires 2√ó bandwidth compared to NRZ

2.Optical Signal Performance Analysis

Full simulation setup: PRBS generator creates test patterns ‚Üí NRZ/RZ modulation ‚Üí laser transmission ‚Üí variable attenuation ‚Üí receiver with noise filtering ‚Üí eye diagram and BER analysis

The Question: How much can we weaken an optical signal before it becomes unreadable?

How I Tested It:

  • Changed one thing: Attenuation (like adding sunglasses to a flashlight)
  • Measured: Signal quality using eye diagrams
  • Compared: Two blinking patterns (NRZ vs. RZ)
  • Found: RZ sees better in the dark but talks twice as fast

Engineering Insight: Every communication system balances signal integrity against bandwidth usage. Sometimes we need clearer vision (RZ), sometimes we need to save bandwidth (NRZ).
